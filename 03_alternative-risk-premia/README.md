# Alternative Risk Premia

This folder contains research on systematic sources of return beyond traditional long-only market exposure.

The focus is on studying whether simple, rule-based strategies can capture persistent return patterns that are better understood as alternative risk premia than as pure alpha. In practice, that means looking at different styles across different markets and asking whether they offer useful diversification, different risk behavior, or more robust portfolio construction when combined. 

The current research is centered on a few broad areas:

- **Trend** in futures markets, where absolute price persistence may be used to manage directional exposure
- **Term structure** in futures markets, where the shape of the curve may contain information about carry or positioning
- **Volatility** in options markets, where option prices may embed a premium for bearing insurance-like risk
- **Momentum** in equity markets, where relative winners and losers may continue to diverge over intermediate horizons

Taken together, these ideas fit naturally within an alternative risk premia framework: they are systematic, cross-market, and intended to be evaluated not only by their standalone returns, but also by how they diversify one another. A central motivation is that combining lowly correlated premia may produce more stable outcomes than relying on a single style or on traditional market exposure alone. 

This folder is still at a research stage. The aim for now is to keep these strategies organized as research prototypes that can later be refined, compared, combined, or extended to other variants within the same framework.
